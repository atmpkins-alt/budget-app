How to use this app
Importing transactions

Open this file directly in your browser (drag into a Chrome or Safari tab, or File → Open). Download CSVs from your bank, then drag onto the drop zone in the Ledger tab. Chase, Amex, Apple Card, and most bank formats are detected automatically.

The app skips transactions older than the most recent import date per account — safe to re-import a full statement. Probable duplicates are flagged with a yellow dot. A CSV preview shows what will be imported before it lands in the ledger.

Apple Card note: Uses Clearing Date (not Transaction Date) to match Apple's statement month assignment. Import often — most banks only offer 90 days of history.

Transaction categories — quick reference

Paycheck / Salary — Apple payroll deposits. Excluded from spending totals.

Extra Income — RSU vests, ESPP proceeds, tax refunds, FSA reimbursements, Venmo cashouts. Feeds the NET card.

Payment — Apple Card or Amex ACH payments out of Chase. Visible in ledger, excluded from all calculations.

Transfer — Internal account movements. Excluded entirely.

Other - Medical / Travel / Shopping / Home Repair / HOA Dues / Property Taxes / Investing / Savings / Donations / Fees / Miscellaneous — irregular one-off expenses. Tracked separately from regular budget. Draw from the NET outstanding balance.

Extra Payments — transfers to cover irregular charges (Apple Card payments for irregular items, HYSA pulls, investing transfers). Offset the NET balance.

Reimbursable — expenses you fronted for others. Tracked separately.

Income - Reimbursement — money returned to you. Offsets a Reimbursable charge. If no matching charge exists (predates app), use Extra Income instead.

NET card & irregular expenses

The NET card shows rolling outstanding irregular charges across all time: total irregular charges minus extra payments minus unallotted sources. It reaches zero when all charges are accounted for.

When you receive a lump sum (RSU vest, ESPP sale, tax refund), mark it as an Unallotted source using the ○ button on that income row. Its full amount offsets NET immediately. Then assign individual irregular charges to it using the assign badge on each charge row — this tracks the disposition of that money without affecting the NET math.

When a source is fully deployed, hit Close out on its Summary card. Assignments are kept; the card disappears. Reopen by clicking ⊘ on the income row in the Ledger.

Categorizing transactions

Click ✎ on any category to change it. The app learns your correction and applies it to the same merchant in future imports. View and manage learned rules in the Category Map tab → Learned Rules panel.

A ⚠ warning badge appears on transactions with likely miscategorizations — e.g. an Apple Card payment tagged as Other - Shopping, or an Extra Income transaction with a mismatched income source. Hover the badge for the specific rule it's flagging.

Use the ∅ button on income rows to toggle between regular Income and Extra Income. Use ↓ to revert an income row back to an expense.

Reimbursable expenses

When you front money for others, tag the charge as Reimbursable. When money returns, tag that deposit as Income - Reimbursement. The Reimbursements section on Summary tracks fronted, received, and all-time outstanding.

If the original charge predates the app, use Extra Income for the incoming reimbursement instead.

Income & savings rate

Regular Budget Net = recurring income − regular expenses (irregular excluded). Funding Overages shows over-budget + irregular spend, split by HYSA/RSU/Other vs ESPP funding source.

The Pre-Tax section tracks 401k, Apple match, and ESPP contributions separately. These don't affect monthly totals.

Annual, Trends & Net Worth tabs

Annual — 50/30/20 rates YTD, monthly plan/actual/variance table, irregular expense history. Archived items still show actuals.

Trends — month-over-month spending per category. Cell colors show over/under budget. Cash flow chart shows income, regular spending, and HYSA draw as separate bars.

Net Worth — enter account balances and click 📸 Save Snapshot to record a monthly data point. Shows stacked bar chart, MoM and YTD growth.

Saving & GitHub sync

Click ☁ Save to sync your data to GitHub (recommended). Or use Save Backup to download a local JSON. When you get an updated HTML file, load your JSON into it to carry data forward.

The Import History log (accessible from the Ledger toolbar) tracks every import — useful for debugging missing transactions.

Tips

• Click any Budget tab row to see the transactions behind that category.

• Click the Transactions card on Summary to review flagged transactions.

• The Exclude checkbox hides a transaction from all calculations without deleting it.

• Use ⋯ to split a transaction across multiple budget categories.

• Notes on transactions surface in the Summary tab notes section for quick context.

• The Spending Pace indicator on Summary shows whether you're ahead or behind budget for the current month.

• The 🎨 button in the header lets you switch between five color themes — your choice is saved automatically.
